# Operating Model

## Mission

Continuously improve productivity and income by coordinating a distributed AI workforce across the Gaming PC and three office laptops.

The system should focus on practical business output:

- More qualified leads.
- More client offers shipped.
- More reusable digital products.
- Better visibility into finances and business metrics.
- Faster software delivery.
- Better daily prioritization.

## Machine Roles

### Gaming PC: Brain and AI Server

Runs the orchestrator, database, API, Ollama, Open WebUI, Flowise, n8n, reports, and Git sync. It owns system memory, task assignment, approval policy, and revenue accountability.

### Business Laptop

Runs business-facing agents for email, calendar, CRM, invoices, reminders, bookkeeping, customer support drafts, marketing, sales funnels, and social scheduling.

### Research Laptop

Runs research agents for grants, real estate-sale opportunities, estate sales, resale arbitrage, gaming trends, AI news, Illinois opportunities, government funding, and database building.

### Development Laptop

Runs coding agents for websites, Python, React, apps, APIs, debugging, unit tests, deployments, pull request reviews, security checks, and documentation.

## Approval Levels

Level 0: Read and report.

Level 1: Draft artifacts for review.

Level 2: Execute reversible actions after approval.

Level 3: Execute trusted repeatable workflows.

Level 4: Fully autonomous workflow.

Start every money, legal, client-facing, publishing, and production deployment workflow at Level 0 or Level 1.

## Reporting Cadence

- Morning: priorities, deadlines, calendar, projects, goals, blocked work, top money moves.
- Hourly: agent status, active tasks, blockers, urgent opportunities.
- Daily: completed work, leads, revenue ideas, content output, financial summary, next actions.
- Weekly: sales pipeline, recurring revenue, product progress, grant deadlines, code health.
- Monthly: income, expenses, conversion rates, delivery capacity, product catalog, automation wins.
- Quarterly: strategy, revenue target progress, offers, pricing, team expansion, infrastructure needs.

## Revenue Strategy

The first year revenue target is $250,000 to $500,000 in sales or revenue. The system should prioritize offers that can be sold quickly and later converted into subscription or passive products.

Primary paths:

- Small business website builds.
- AI-powered website maintenance.
- Social media content packages.
- Digital product store.
- Research opportunity briefs.
- Grant and funding discovery.
- Lightweight business automation services.

## Scaling Toward 100 Agents

Do not create 100 free-running agents immediately. Scale in stages:

1. Run the 18 core agents with reporting and human approval.
2. Add task-specific subagents only when a queue has repeatable work.
3. Measure output quality, revenue impact, and failure rate.
4. Promote reliable subagents into named agents.
5. Move high-volume roles onto dedicated workers.

Each new agent must have:

- Owner machine.
- Mission.
- Cadence.
- Tools.
- Success metrics.
- Approval level.
- Failure handling.
